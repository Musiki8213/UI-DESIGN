Business Tech: Then vs. Now



Communication

Then: In 2000, most communication happened over landline phones, fax machines, or slow email threads.
Now: Today, instant messaging apps like Slack and video calls via Zoom allow for real-time, seamless collaboration.`;

Hardware and Devices

Then: Desks were cluttered with CRT monitors, fax machines, and flip phones.
Now: Modern workspaces feature sleek laptops, smartphones, and minimalist setups with wireless accessories.

Software and Tools

Then: Businesses relied on offline software like Microsoft Office installed from CDs, with limited integration.
Now: Cloud-based platforms like Google Workspace and AI-powered tools streamline workflows from anywhere.

Data and Storage

Then: Files were stored in physical cabinets or saved on floppy disks and CDs, prone to damage or loss.
Now: Cloud storage provides secure, real-time access to files from any device, anywhere in the world.
